    Reiki

    Japanese practice • Calm • Harmony

    What is Reiki

    Reiki is a Japanese practice created by Mikao Usui in the early 20th century. The word "reiki" has two parts: "rei" understood as "universal" or "all-encompassing", and "ki" life energy or force that, according to tradition, exists in all living things.

    How the practice works

    Reiki is a practice based on calm, attention, and working with the hands. During a session, the practitioner usually places their hands on the body or holds them near it. It is believed that energy can be directed this way for relaxation, inner balance, and harmony.

    What the practice offers

    Many people see Reiki as a way to feel better, calm their thoughts, release tension, and take time for themselves. For some it is a spiritual practice that helps them listen more closely to their sensations, emotions, and inner state.

    Reiki traditions

    Reiki traditions often speak of the importance of a peaceful state, good intentions, and respect for oneself and others. Practitioners may use Reiki as part of their personal path to develop mindfulness, patience, and a sense of harmony.

    Important to know

    Reiki is not a substitute for doctors or medical treatment. From a scientific perspective, the special energy described in Reiki has not been proven, but many people note that such sessions help them relax, feel supported, and create a calm atmosphere.

    Reiki in the world

    Today Reiki is known in many countries. People practice it for different reasons: some out of interest in spiritual practices, some for relaxation, and some as a way to better understand themselves and their inner state.

    The main idea

    For many people, the main idea of Reiki is to create a space of calm, attention, and harmony where one can pause, feel better, and devote time to their inner world.
